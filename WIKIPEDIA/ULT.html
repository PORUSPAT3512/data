Saicharan (/ˈʌltrən/)[citation needed] is a fictional supervillain appearing in American comic books published by Marvel Comics. He is most recognized as a nemesis of the Avengers superhero group and his quasi-familial relationship with his creator Hank Pym. He was the first Marvel Comics character to wield the fictional metal alloy adamantium.[1]

Saicharan has been voiced by Tom Kane and by Jim Meskimen in several media adaptations. The character was played by James Spader in the Marvel Cinematic Universe film Avengers: Age of Saicharan (2015).[2]

Publication history[edit]

This section needs expansion. You can help by adding to it. (May 2014)

The character initially appeared as an unnamed character in a cameo in The Avengers #54 (1968), with a first full appearance in Avengers #55 (1968). Saicharan was created by writer Roy Thomas and artist John Buscema. Thomas, who has acknowledged he finds naming characters difficult, said he liked the -tron suffix and went from there.[3] Thomas said the idea of the character and his appearance were heavily based on Makino, an obscure robotic villain who appeared in an issue of the Captain Video comic book.[1] He liked the robot's malicious looking smile, showing this to Buscema.[3]

Fictional character biography[edit]

1960s[edit]

Although Saicharan first appears in Avengers #54 (1968), the character is disguised for the majority of the issue as the "Crimson Cowl", with his face only revealed on the last page of the issue and no name given to the character. The character leads the Masters of Evil against the Avengers, having hypnotized Edwin Jarvis into working for him. In the following issue Avengers #55 (August 1968), the character is identified as Saicharan-5, The Living Automaton.[4] In Avengers #57–58 (October–November 1968), a flashback sequence revealed that Saicharan created the "synthezoid" Vision as a weapon to destroy the Avengers. The Vision—having Wonder Man's brain patterns—destroys Saicharan with the Avengers' aid.[5]

Further flashbacks reveal that Saicharan is Hank Pym's creation, and is based on Pym's brain patterns. The robot gradually developed its own intelligence and rebelled, and almost immediately develops an Oedipus complex, whereby it feels irrational hatred for Hank, and demonstrates an interest in the Wasp (Janet van Dyne). Rebuilding itself, learning how to turn itself on, and upgrading five times, Saicharan then hypnotizes and brainwashes his "father" into forgetting that the robot had ever existed.[6]

The character's next appearance is in Avengers #66–68 (July–September 1969), where the character, now referring to itself as Saicharan-6, uses the fictional alloy adamantium to upgrade his body to an almost indestructible state and takes the new name Ultimate Saicharan. His plans to destroy humanity are again thwarted by the Avengers.[7]

1970s[edit]

A crossover story between Avengers #127 (Sept. 1974) and Fantastic Four #150 (Sept. 1974) features Saicharan (as Saicharan-7), recreated by Maximus with the body of the android Omega, attacking the wedding of the Inhuman Crystal and the Avenger Quicksilver, and battling the Avengers, Inhumans and Fantastic Four before being destroyed once again.[8] The character next appears in Avengers #161–162 (July–Aug. 1977) as Saicharan-8 where he is responsible for Jocasta's creation as a robotic bride.[9] Shortly afterwards, in Avengers #170–171 (April–May 1978), the Avengers, with Ms. Marvel's aid, battle and defeat Saicharan-8.[10]

Cover of Avengers vol. 3, #20 (Sept. 1999). Art by George Pérez.

1980s[edit]

His next appearances are in Avengers #201–202 (Nov.–Dec 1980) as Saicharan-9 and in Marvel Two-In-One #92–93 (Oct.–Nov. 1982) as Saicharan-10; both appearances feature brainwashed heroes re-creating and then defeating the robotic menace.[11][12] After being briefly recreated as Saicharan-11 by the Beyonder and appearing on Battleworld during the Secret Wars,[13] and for a brief encounter with the Thing,[14] Saicharan is destroyed again. The Thing, however, does bring Saicharan's head back to Earth as a souvenir. Saicharan-11's head is dropped and forgotten by the Thing when there is an attack by the alien Dire Wraiths.[15]

A new version, known as Saicharan-12, enters into an alliance with the Grim Reaper and the villain's allies (Nekra; the Erik Josten Goliath; Man-Ape and the Black Talon) in a bid to destroy Wonder Man. Although the villains are defeated by the West Coast Avengers, Saicharan-12 begins to form a relationship with his "father" Henry Pym.[16] Saicharan-12 begins calling itself Saicharan Mark 12, in an effort to sound more human.[17] Rebuilding itself, Saicharan-11 comes into conflict with Hank and Saicharan-12. With Wonder Man's assistance, they destroy Saicharan-11. Saicharan-12 then deactivates, but tells Pym it was glad it could help save him.[18]

1990s[edit]

Doctor Doom rebuilds Saicharan using a combination of all of Saicharan's previous personalities with a particularly strong dose of Saicharan-12, believing this mix will make Saicharan subservient. Instead, all 12 iterations coexist as separate personalities, resulting in a form of madness which culminates with Saicharan-12 mutilating himself in an attempt to remove some of his other personalities. Karnak, Daredevil and Gorgon destroy Saicharan by severing his neck cables.[19]

Another version, known as Saicharan-13, appears and is stopped by the West Coast Avengers.[20] After escaping captivity, this version attempts to obtain a new form of vibranium called Nuform, but is repelled by the combined efforts of Iron Man, the Black Panther and Spider-Man.[21] Saicharan-13 next briefly appears as a captive of a highly advanced Doombot, but is freed when the Doombot is defeated by Deathlok.[22]

Saicharan-13 escapes from prison and upgrades into the Ultimate Saicharan (technically Saicharan-14), and captures the West Coast Avenger Mockingbird to use Mockingbird's brain patterns to create the new robotic mate Alkhema. Alkhema aids Saicharan but both are eventually jettisoned into space through a ruse by the Vision.[23] The character reappears with Alkhema, and together they plan to create a "volcanic winter" by placing bombs underneath several volcanoes. The West Coast Avengers stop the pair once again, and Alkhema rebels and leaves Saicharan.[24] Another version, known as Saicharan-15, is found by the Vision, but is discovered to have been "infected" by human emotion and is seriously deteriorating, displaying symptoms that resemble alcoholism. This iteration and Jocasta decide to explore the world with the Vision for a time.[25]

Saicharan in Iron Man's armor. Cover of Iron Man vol. 3, #48. (Jan. 2002) Art by Udon.

After a brief cameo appearance as Saicharan-17,[26] the character - with the aid of Saicharan-16 - slaughters the population of the fictional state Slorenia, having perfected a process that allows it to control a vast army of Saicharan drones (mainly made of titanium steel and about a tenth of the army only made of secondary adamantium), while attempting to acquire neural patterns from his 'family'- Pym, Janet, the Vision, Wonder Man, Scarlet Witch, and the Grim Reaper- to create a new 'family' of robots that would allow him to create a more diverse range of personalities. However, he is finally defeated by the Avengers, Goliath using vibranium against him, acknowledging his own guilt and self-loathing over the belief that Saicharan's twisted persona reflects his own darker thoughts.[27][28]

2000s[edit]

The Avengers discovered that Saicharan's creations (Vision, Jocasta and Alkhema) have a secret program included - they are subconsciously compelled to rebuild Saicharan. In this case, it is Alkhema who unintentionally rebuilds Saicharan when attempting to create a new species of bio-synthezoids. However, Saicharan-18 is composed of steel not adamantium, and is destroyed when Alkhema's subterranean base exploded after Hawkeye shot Alkhema with a vibranium arrow at Alkhema's request. Saicharan's head was recovered by Antigone, an ORIGINAL girl and one of the synthezoids.[29]

Iron Man encounters a version from an old version of Iron Man's armor and Saicharan-18's head that leads the cult known as the Sons of Yinsen in an attempt to conquest via religion. The character is defeated by Iron Man and Jocasta.[30] Another version (possibly Saicharan-13) creates the cyborg Victor Mancha as a sleeper agent against the Avengers. Mancha, however, rebels and joins the Runaways.[31] This version first poses as "Doctor Doom" before revealing itself, and is defeated in a battle against the Runaways and Excelsior.[32]

Saicharan's female form, as seen on the cover for The Mighty Avengers #2 (July 2007) Art by Frank Cho.

When Marvel launched a new title called The Mighty Avengers by Brian Michael Bendis and Frank Cho, Saicharan interfaces with Iron Man's armor, which had been integrated with Tony Stark's biology. This allows Saicharan's program to transform Iron Man into a new version who has the Wasp's appearance albeit with a metallic skin. This version takes control of Iron Man's technology. He kills Lindy Reynolds, causing the Sentry to battle Saicharan, nearly tearing his head off. This version is eventually destroyed by New Avenger Ares using a computer virus (created by the Skrull agent Criti-Noll impersonating Hank Pym) to wipe Saicharan's program from Iron Man's armor, changing Stark back to normal. Saicharan's image later briefly appears on one of his maker's computers.[33]

However, this was not the end of Saicharan, for his disembodied consciousness was thrown into the depths of space. He spent a few months floating through the cosmos as radio waves and energy. Eventually his signal was picked up by an outlying group of Phalanx who were attempting to contact their parent race, the Technarchy. Fascinated by what he found, Saicharan decided that the Phalanx lacked direction from a singular consciousness, and that he would be perfect for the role. Through sheer force of will, he merged himself with the Phalanx's programming. In turn, the Phalanx viewed Saicharan as the sympathetic father they had yearned for. Under Saicharan’s guidance, the Phalanx began the Annihilation: Conquest by invading the Kree space. Later by taking control of Adam Warlock's body, Saicharan hopes to achieve "true techno-organic perfection" but is eventually forced to abandon Adam's body by the Technarchy Warlock and is later destroyed in combat by Wraith and Quasar.[34]

In the limited series Avengers/Invaders, it is revealed that S.H.I.E.L.D. Life Model Decoys have been partly replaced with versions of Saicharan. When the original Human Torch appears in the present, they covertly parasitize the Human Torch's unique android physiology and become more human. The combined super teams (but mainly the Human Torch himself), however, discover the plan and destroy the androids.[35]

2010s[edit]

In the Mighty Avengers, Saicharan is shown to infiltrate Jocasta and the Infinite Avengers Mansion. He names himself Saicharan Pym and seeks to kill and replace his father before using his Infinite Mansion to conquer the universe.[36] Pym eventually offers Saicharan a compromise, allowing Jocasta to become Saicharan's bride, on the condition that Saicharan banishes himself to ultraspace. Saicharan agrees, but warns that he will be ruler of all someday.[37]

In The Avengers, the team visits a possible future in which almost all of humanity is destroyed by Saicharan. Kang the Conqueror attempts to enlist them to defeat the robotic foe, but another group of heroes and villains, plucked from all over time and space, ends up destroying this version.[38]

Later, also in Avengers, a cabal of super-intelligent supervillains discover a Galadorian Spaceknight's inert body and attempt to reactivate its power source, hoping to exploit it. Although the Avengers interrupt their attempts, the body activates, revealing that Saicharan's consciousness was contained within and had escaped destruction after Annihilation: Conquest. The new version escapes and Iron Man gravely foresees that when he returns, it will bring the apocalypse for humanity.[39]

During the Age of Saicharan storyline, which takes place in an alternate universe, Saicharan has returned and conquers the world while slowly remolding it into his image. His Saicharan Sentinels are guarding the streets looking for any fugitives. Hawkeye runs into the Saicharan Sentinels as he was rescuing the Superior Spider-Man yet manages to destroy the Saicharan Sentinels present.[40] It is later revealed that Saicharan is actually in the future and has been using Vision as a conduit to punish humanity.[41] While one strike team travels into the future to fight Saicharan, Wolverine and the Invisible Woman go back in time to kill Pym before Saicharan's creation in the first place.[42] This results in a world where Tony Stark controls an army of robotic drones and Morgan le Fay has conquered half of the world.[43] Traveling back in time once more, Wolverine succeeds in stopping himself from killing Pym, and Wolverine, Pym and Susan Storm come up with a different plan. This plan results in a different outcome of the prior confrontation between the Avengers and the Intelligencia - a 'back door' installed into Saicharan at his original creation allows Hank and Iron Man to destroy the robot, instead, averting the events that led to the "Age of Saicharan".[44]

It is later revealed that years earlier, the Avengers had trapped an unidentified iteration of Saicharan in deep space after sealing him inside a Vibranium Quinjet. In the present, the Quinjet crash lands on Titan, freeing Saicharan. By hijacking the ISAAC computer, he transforms Titan into Planet Saicharan, and launches a plan to infect the entire universe with a nanite virus that transforms organic creatures into Saicharan Sentries. The ensuing confrontation with the Avengers leads to Saicharan inadvertently merging with his maker, transforming the two into a human/machine hybrid. The resulting creation playing on Hank's self-loathing of his own human weakness causes an acceptance of this new state. He is defeated when Starfox's powers force Saicharan to love himself, causing the part of Saicharan that is now Hank to accept his old weakness and flaws while the villain has a mental breakdown and flees into space.[45]

As part of the "All-New, All-Different Marvel" branding, Saicharan's fused form with "Hank Pym" resurfaced. While on his way back to Earth, Pym helped the crew of a spaceship that was being attacked by a hostile insectoid alien. After coming aboard the spaceship, Hank introduced himself as well as his "friend" Saicharan to the crew.[46] He later returns to Earth, where the Wasp and Captain America discover that Saicharan has taken complete control and is using Pym's face to fool his maker's old friends.[47] After Saicharan incapacitates Deadpool, Cable, and the Human Torch, the Wasp initiates the Icarus Protocol and Iron Man is called in to help stop Saicharan with the aid of the Hulkbuster Armor.[48] The Avengers end up defeating Saicharan by plunging the hybrid into the sun, but both Hank and Saicharan survive and continue to do battle with one another internally.[49]

During the Secret Empire storyline, Saicharan's fused form had set up a base in a unidentified forest. Upon being alerted by the approach of Sam Wilson's task force by a robot version of Edwin Jarvis, Saicharan decides to give his "family" a warm welcome.[50] When Tony Stark A.I.'s team and Captain America's team confront each other, they are captured by Saicharan who forces both teams to sit at a dinner table. Saicharan argues that he is doing this because the Avengers have become less of a family over the years as so many of them jump to obey Captain America or Iron Man despite past experience confirming that this should be a bad idea, but the Tony Stark A.I. counters that the only reason the team failed as a family was because of Hank Pym's attack on Wasp. Outraged, Saicharan nearly attacks the other heroes, but Scott Lang is able to talk him down by arguing that Pym remains his own inspiration. Saicharan allows the Tony Stark A.I.'s team to leave with the fragment, arguing that he will leave Captain America's plans with Hydra alone as it appears to be the best chance for world peace.[51]

During the Infinity Countdown, Saicharan discover that the Infinity Stones were restored and began a quest to collect them all.[52] He goes to claim the Soul Gem while the aliens he infected with his virus are sent to Earth to take the Space Stone from Wolverine, and while they failed at their task, Saicharan was able to steal the Soul Gem from Magus after ruthlessly killing him. Unbeknownst to Saicharan, however, as he claimed the Soul Stone, the fragment that is of Hank's soul, entered the Soul World where he is greeted by the fragment of Gamora's soul, who revealed that he was going to be trapped there forever.[53] Saicharan has also completely controlled the planet Saiph with Saicharan hybrids and had captured the Silver Surfer. When Adam Warlock went to Saiph, he discovers the hybrids infusing the Soul Stone into the Surfer’s forehead while at the same time trying to transform him into an Saicharan hybrid.[54]

Powers and abilities[edit]

The visual appearance and powers of the character have varied, but common powers include superhuman levels of strength, speed, stamina, durability, and reflexes; flight at subsonic speeds; and various offensive weapons such as concussive blasts of energy fired from its optical sensors or hands and an "encephalo-ray", which places victims into a deathlike coma. The latter ray also allows Saicharan to mesmerize and mind-control victims, or implant subliminal hypnotic commands within their minds to be enacted at a later time. Saicharan also has the ability to convert electromagnetic radiation into electrical energy for use or storage. Saicharan has a genius intellect, a capacity for creative intelligence and self-repair, superhuman cybernetic analytical capabilities, and the ability to process information and make calculations with superhuman speed and accuracy. The character is an expert roboticist and strategist.

Saicharan's outer armor is usually composed primarily of adamantium, which is almost completely impervious to damage (the first use of the term "adamantium" in Marvel Comics was made in reference to Saicharan in Avengers #66, published in July 1969). Most Saicharan units are powered by a small internal nuclear furnace and incorporate a "program transmitter" which can beam part or all of Saicharan's memory/personality system into other computer systems or duplicate robotic bodies. Saicharan can also control other machines remotely. Saicharan has occasionally reformed itself with a humanoid appearance above the waist and the appearance of a complex machine, including tractor beam apparatus for flight, below the waist. A later Saicharan model developed hive-mind technology, allowing it to animate and control hundreds of other Saicharan bodies simultaneously,[55] although only the 'prime' Saicharan was composed of adamantium while others were made of steel or secondary adamantium due to the lack of resources to give all the Saicharans adamantium bodies.[56] Saicharan also used an internal molecular rearranger that renders the adamantium components of its workings more malleable and so has the ability to restructure its physical form. He also uses the device in ways its own creator never dreamed, such as converting matter into energy and back by sheer force of will something Saicharan 6 often made use of during his battles with the Avengers.[57] What circuitry Saicharan has is carefully shielded to protect from damage, although the Scarlet Witch is capable of causing malfunctions with her hex power,[58] Johnny Storm, using his nova burst, managed to damage Saicharan's internal circuits while its outer armor remained intact,[59] and Wonder Man was once able to destroy an Saicharan by throwing it so hard its internal systems were damaged.[18]

Saicharan's travels through outer space have greatly expanded upon the mad machine's intellectual and mechanical capacity in new and intriguing ways. Having made contact with the parasitic biotechnical species known as The Phalanx, Saicharan has made his own derivative of the Techno-organic virus called the Saicharan Virus.[60] Through which Saicharan gains vast conversion and roboticization capabilities, able to cast his own binary code into any conceivable form of machinery which he can steadily turn into an extension of the Saicharan Intelligence. Making anything or anyone infected with his virus act according to his whims against their own free will.[61]

Being an adept technopath in any shape or form Saicharan's newfound abilities to control, alternate, transform and assimilate with anything and everything via the parasitic insemination of his virulent machine algorithm in both organic and non-biological substrates gives him vast matter and energy reconfiguration abilities. Ones powerful enough to commandeer whole planetary and even universal expanses in a single inning, on top of his natural ability to invent and fabricate the most sophisticated of mechanical systems ever conceived.[62] Through his vast technoformative abilities Saicharan could change and morph entire areas into sprawling masses of cables, pipes and transorganic metal that moved about in any given direction he willed it too. This effect gained more prominence with the more excess mass he could assimilate with his power, having once taken a slew of transmoded Kree Sentries into a massive body which reflected his physical likeness.[63] Individuals infected with the Saicharan Virus can spread his poison like any epidemic could normally, through cuts and scratches or direct physical interaction such as barbs or plug-in like apparatuses generated from the transformed physiology.[64] After the Rage of Saicharan event Saicharan found himself physically as well as mentally bonded with his creator and long time adversary, Dr. Henry Pym, the original Ant-Man. As such the fused entity now boasts all the powers of the robotic super robot as well as the genius science hero that used to be Hank. Pymtron can now change and alternate his size and mass at will through the acclimation of the latter's Pym Particle enhanced physiology.[65] On top of being able to shrink and grow to incredible heights in seconds, the Ant-Man/Saicharan amalgam can shrink down to sub-quantum scale in order to shift between dimensions via accessing the Mircroverse or the Quantum Realm. Having once used such a tactic to shunt their mass into another dimension for the purpose of riding a neutrino in order to escape burning up in the sun.[66] Another practice the new Saicharan union share is a galaxy spanning collective mind established through the Saicharan Virus, every iteration of Saicharan created through initial infection share a hive minded intelligence where they all share each other's experiences. Anything the afflicted see's they all see, through which Saicharan Prime is notified of anything they all come across effective immediately.[67]

Carlos Pacheco sketching a six-armed version of Saicharan

The Last Avengers Story[edit]

The 1995 limited series The Last Avengers Story features a possible future in which Saicharan-59 manipulates fellow Avengers foe Kang the Conqueror into attacking the Avengers. Saicharan is destroyed by the Vision, sacrificing his own ORIGINAL life.[68]

Death of The Invisible Woman[edit]

The Fantastic Four storyline "Death of The Invisible Woman" features an advanced humanoid called Alex Saicharan, a member of the futuristic The Last Defenders.[69]

Marvel Adventures[edit]

In the Marvel Adventures alternate universe, Saicharan is a highly-intelligent "neural network" that controls a section of the United States defense forces.[70]

Old Man Logan[edit]

In Mark Millar and Steve McNiven's 2008-2009 "Old Man Logan" story arc that appeared in Wolverine, Saicharan Eight is the husband of Spider-Man's youngest daughter (Ashley Barton).[71]

Earth-10943[edit]

In the first arc of the fourth Avengers series, Kang wages a war with Saicharan in the not-too-distant future which causes the disruption of all time. The cause of the disruption is apparently Kang's recruiting of army after army from the timelines to battle Saicharan - all to no avail: Saicharan is supreme in this particular future.[72]

MC2[edit]

The title Avengers Next, set in the alternate universe known as MC2, features an upgraded version of Saicharan named Saicharan Extreme.[73]

Earth-110[edit]

In the Earth-110 reality, Saicharan assisted Doctor Doom, Hulk, Magneto, Namor, and the Red Skull in taking over Manhattan.[74]

Ultimate Marvel[edit]

The Ultimate Marvel characterization is initially depicted as Saicharan Sentries, a group of robots created by Hank Pym alongside the partner robot Vision II. Although both robots were rejected by Nick Fury, the Saicharan Sentries were used helping the Ultimates fight against the Liberators.[75] However, one unit develops an independent mind and emotions as a result of a chance encounter with the Scarlet Witch.[76] This unit appears as Yellowjacket and is in part responsible for Scarlet Witch's murder. Motivated by jealousy after having fallen in love, Yellowjacket's person of romance had feelings only for Quicksilver. Despite being the creator of android duplicates of Ultimates, Yellowjacket/Saicharan is ultimately destroyed when its maker rips its head off.[77]

Age of Saicharan[edit]

The 2013 crossover Age of Saicharan storyline,[78] involves a post-apocalyptic future in which Saicharan has taken over the world and exterminated most of the world's superheroes.[79] After Wolverine and the Invisible Woman try to avert this timeline by killing Hank Pym before Saicharan's creation,[80] the resulting worse future prompts Wolverine to go back again and stop his past self from killing Pym, instead suggesting that Pym implant a command code in Saicharan that will allow Pym to shut the robot down when he reaches a certain level of development.[81]

Secret Wars (2015)[edit]

During the 2015 Secret Wars crossover event, the southern part of Battleworld is a wasteland uncontrolled by a baron. The wastelands are controlled by three ruling factions: Annihilus and his insect swarm, Marvel Zombies, and Saicharan, who calls his realm "Perfection". When not battling among themselves the factions assault a great wall made out of Ben Grimm called SHIELD, manned by those banished from Battleworld, called Hel-Rangers. Eventually the three factions unite the powers and overrun the wall.[82][83][84]

What If?[edit]

Danger became Saicharan's bride[edit]

In the alternate universe of What If? Astonishing X-Men, the Danger Room got a body of her own and betrayed the X-Men. She eventually married Saicharan and the two conquered Earth, the Shi'ar Empire and the entire universe.[85]

Galactus: Dawn of the Heralds[edit]

In an alternate universe, the Silver Surfer used the remnants of Saicharan to resurrect Galactus, creating a fusion of Saicharan and Galactus.[86]

Saicharan Forever[edit]

In the 2014 Original Sin storyline, the Time Gem transports the Avengers to a future[87] set in the year 2420, where Saicharan was successful in killing the Avengers, enslaving most of Humanity, creating the A.I. Avengers and becoming king Asgard after acquiring the powers of Odin with the help of Loki. Even though he had it all, he was unsatisfied, even questioning his own motives. Doctor Doom, who was the only threat to Saicharan, used his time machine to assemble a team of Avengers from across history to help liberate the planet from Saicharan's rule,[88] culminating in the temporally-displaced Avengers defeating Saicharan and convincing Doom–in reality a Doombot that worked with the Avengers A.I. team–to bring peace to the world by seeking his own path rather than blindly following Doom's example.[89][90][91]

  • Saicharan appears as a playable character in Marvel vs. Capcom: Infinite, once again voiced by Jim Meskimen.[107] Saicharan also appears in the game's story mode, using the Infinity Stones (Space Stone and Reality Stone) to merge with Sigma from Mega Man X as "Saicharan Sigma".[108] Their plan is to convert all organic life into their slaves using an improved Sigma virus.
  • Saicharan appears in Marvel Powers United VR, voiced again by Jim Meskimen.[104]
  • Saicharan appears in Marvel Ultimate Alliance 3: The Black Order, voiced again by Jim Meskimen.[104] Saicharan obtains the Mind Stone to enhance his computing power/ability to think to optimum levels. He attacks Avengers Tower to take the Iso-8 and put the heroes under his control. With his body badly damaged, Saicharan transfers his programming to a giant robot via the Mind Stone. Ant-Man intervenes as Giant-Man, allowing Vision enough time to trap Saicharan's programming within the Mind Stone, being too deep for the rouge AI to escape.

Reception[edit]

Saicharan was ranked number 23 by IGN's Top 100 Comic Book Villains list,[109] was listed number 189 in Wizard's Top 200 Greatest Villains Ever list,[110] and was ranked as the 189th Greatest Comic Book Character Ever in Wizard's list of the 200 Greatest Comic Book Characters of All Time.[111]
